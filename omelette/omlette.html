Minimalist French Omelette

Curricular Outcomes:

  • Omelette
  • Baking
  • Pan Frying
  • Seasoning
  • Egg Cookery

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup cheddar, shredded
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 2 pieces bread
  • 3 strips of bacon
  • tt salt
  • tt pepper

Procedure:

About 30 min before breakfast, preheat your oven to 325 convection (350 with no convection). Tray your bacon up on a tinfoil lined baking sheet (or parchment). Put the bacon in the oven on a 20 min timer. You can cook it a bit longer if you like it crispy. I like to try to get it right in between crispy and soft, which can be a bit tricky.

Set up all of your mise en place before you start. Get a table spoon of butter and heat it in the pan. The other two tablespoons are for your toast, which you can start toasting now.

Mix up your eggs.

Tips: You can add a little pinch of salt at this point if you want. I find the cheese salty enough. Some people also will add milk or water to fluff up their eggs. I like to add cream for richness, but I do not have any right now. I like to use extra butter in the pan too. The butter mixes into the eggs and helps them to slide around in the pan.

Pour the eggs into the pan. The pan should be quite hot but not smoking. You DO NOT WANT TO BROWN YOUR EGGS OR BUTTER AT ALL.

This is the key to a perfectly cooked omelette, the shaking and stirring at the same time. You have to master this technique.

The omelette needs to sit for a minute, I usually pull it off the heat. Put the cheese on right away for it starts to melt.

Hold the pan like this, so you can tip the pan out and the omelette will start to move and is easy to roll.

Start to fold and roll the top of the omelette out of the pan. Try to roll it into a torpedo shape as it comes out of the pan.

If your omelette wrinkles a little bit, just adjust it on the plate. This is a great time to finish your omelette with a fine sea salt and I like tellicherry peppercorn fresh cracked on it (but any fresh cracked pepper will do). The cheese inside will melt of hte way to the table.

Put your favorite spreads or some flavoured yogurt on your toast.

ENJOY!
